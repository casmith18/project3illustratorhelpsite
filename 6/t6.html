Tutorial 6: Introduction to Layers

Why This Matters

As your design gets more complex, things can start to overlap or get accidentally moved around. That’s where layers come in. Layers let you organize everything so you’re not constantly clicking the wrong thing. Think of them like invisible folders stacked on top of each other.

Step 1: Open the Layers Panel

Go to Window > Layers if you don’t already see it on the right side of your screen. You’ll probably see a single layer named “Layer 1” with all your shapes and text inside it.

Screenshot suggestion: Layers panel open with all elements listed under one layer.

Step 2: Create New Layers for Organization

Click the plus icon at the bottom of the Layers panel to add a new layer. Name one “Text,” another “Images,” and maybe another one for “Design Elements.” You can name them anything that helps you remember what’s inside.

Now drag your objects from the default layer into the new ones. This separates your content into manageable parts.

Screenshot suggestion: Multiple named layers in the panel with objects assigned.

Step 3: Lock and Hide Layers

If you’re not working on a specific part of the layout, it’s smart to lock or hide that layer. Just click the lock icon or the eye icon next to the layer name.

This prevents you from accidentally moving or changing something while adjusting other parts of the spread.

Screenshot suggestion: One layer hidden, another locked, and active layer highlighted.

Step 4: Reorder Layers to Change What’s on Top

If an object is hidden behind another and you want to bring it forward, you can either right-click and choose Arrange > Bring to Front or just move it to a higher layer.

This is useful for overlapping text and images or creating depth in your layout.

Screenshot suggestion: Object being dragged into a different layer to change stacking order.
