Adding and Editing Text

Why This Matters

Text is the heart of any magazine layout. It’s what tells the story, gives the reader something to focus on, and balances out the visuals. Illustrator gives you a lot of control over how text looks and behaves, even though it’s not a traditional word processor. In this step, we’ll place headline and body text into the spread layout you created earlier.

Step 1: Add a Headline

Select the Type Tool (T) from the toolbar on the left, then click near the top of your left page. This will be your headline. Type in something that fits the style of your magazine!

Now go to the Properties panel and play with the font, size, and spacing. Pick a bold, readable font. Try setting the font size between 60 and 100 pt depending on how dramatic you want it. Make sure to have your text highlighted in order to make changes. You can do this by tripple clicking inside the text box.

Headline text placed on the left page with large font selected.

Step 2: Add Paragraph Text to the Right Page

Still using the Type Tool, click and drag inside one of the text column boxes you made earlier. This creates a text area, which wraps text automatically inside the shape. Paste in placeholder text like a paragraph of Lorem Ipsum or your own draft content.

Do this for all three columns if you want a full article look.

Text filled inside column boxes using area text.

Step 3: Style the Text

With the text selected, use the Properties panel to adjust the font style, line spacing, and size. Keep the body text smaller—around 10 to 12 pt. You can adjust spacing between lines (called “leading”) and between letters (called “tracking”) to make the text easier to read.

Keep things clean and consistent across the columns.
